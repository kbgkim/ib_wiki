# Investment Banking (IB) Overview

Investment Banking serves as the critical bridge between entities needing capital (Corporations, Governments) and those with capital to invest (Institutional Investors, Private Equity).

## 1. Core Functions of IB

### A. Capital Raising (Underwriting)
- **Equity Capital Markets (ECM)**: IPOs, Follow-on offerings, Rights issues.
- **Debt Capital Markets (DCM)**: Corporate bonds, Government bonds, Structured debt.

### B. Advisory Services
- **M&A (Mergers & Acquisitions)**: Buy-side/Sell-side advisory, LBOs, Divestitures.
- **Restructuring**: Advising distressed companies on capital structure and bankruptcy.

### C. Trading & Principal Investments
- **Market Making**: Providing liquidity in various securities.
- **Principal Investments**: Investing the bank's own capital (Proprietary Trading/Merchant Banking).

## 2. The IB Value Chain
1. **Origination**: Identifying and pitching to clients.
2. **Execution**: Valuation, Due Diligence, Marketing, and Closing.
3. **Syndication**: Distributing the risk/securities to external investors.

## 3. Integrated IB Domain (NPL / PF / Equity / ABS)

In modern specialized IB, these four pillars are deeply interconnected:

| Domain | Key Focus | Integration Point |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Equity** | Ownership & Growth | The ultimate exit for PF or restructured NPL assets. |
| **PF (Project Finance)** | Asset-based financing | Long-term capital for infrastructure; can become NPL if stressed. |
| **NPL (Non-Performing Loan)** | Distressed debt | Opportunities for recovery or restructuring into new Equity/ABS. |
| **ABS (Asset-Backed Securities)** | Liquidity & Securitization | Converting PF loans or NPL portfolios into tradable tranches. |
